Shaming

How would you go against shaming in the office?

A card game to share around in the office

Together with Laurentiu we designed a card game which gives you the opportunity to express yourself without putting the spotlight onto yourself. This creates an environment where you're not put into an uncomfortable spot, but you're still showing other people what your thought process is.

With this approach we want to enable colleagues to express themselves, and if there's a potential bully, you would be able to let them know how you feel about their thought process. Obviously there's no shame in the way people think and they have their own right to their opinion, this merely enables them to talk about it and potentially avoid unnecessary conflict between colleagues.

Personal reflection

We shared the card game around and it showed that people were interested in something like this. To me it was a way to create scenario's people have been in before, and that people all have a different opinion on. I played the card game with my classmates and it shows that people have a different way to do things and that their thought process is a lot different than mine. I believe this was a really useful subject for me because it enabled me to think about how other people feel about bullying. We discussed with my classmates how they have been through these scenarios and how that made them feel.

Design probes

What are the types of online shaming you've experienced?

Online shaming/bullying examples

This whiteboard is a way to make people choose what is shaming/bullying and what is not. Since there's no way people can trace you, it's all anonymous and you can just express your opinion with full honesty. As a group we looked up examples of people getting shamed or bullied, these are the ones we've personally seen online.

The shaming example i gave was the one about Arnold Schwarzenegger. His comment was put out of context which made it so that he got "cancelled". He expressed that people should not have freedom and said "Screw your freedom." This of course stirred controversy because it's in America. What he said in reality was that people should NOT get the freedom to put other peoples health at risk, they should stay inside, get vaccinated and keep their masks on. This of course is a very different message than the above stated.

Personal reflection

As someone who doesn't go through social media very often, this one came up to my by chance. It struck me as really disgusting that people would go to such an extent to make someone look bad/ get bad reputation. There's a lot of people that get shamed or bullied without actually being able to express the real situation. My classmates had an example where a tennis player got outed because they quit early because of personal reasons ( stress related ). This got her a ton of backlash and people started calling her names, which is unacceptable. From this i learned that people love to judge without knowing the entire story.

Vega collab

Creating awareness for bullying

Keep the ball in your court

We wanted to take the approach of showing that you have control over how much power a bully has. By showing everyone that you have a say in how someone gets effected by a bully, we can have a better environment for everyone.

To prevent bullying, we'll have to get to the bottom of bullying, the causes, effects and everything that comes with it, we'll do our research.
